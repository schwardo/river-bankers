Meet the River Bankers: Beavers, otters, muskrats, minks. Four rival groups of animals fighting to collect resources and build structures along the riverbank.

Cards drift from the headwaters through four river spaces. Each shows a row of icons - logs, stones, reeds, mud, vines, or clay.

On your turn, you take one action and pay its cost in fish. Whoever sits furthest back on the fish track acts next.

The main action is a multi-unit auction — using a new mechanic designed specifically for FCC spectrum auctions. Watch.

I pay 2 fish to auction this Reed Stand. The card stays put while we bid. Everyone picks secretly — anywhere from zero up to the five uncovered icons.

Bids are revealed at the same time. I bid three. The otter bids one.

First we both pay our fish — at the Headwaters that's 1 fish per icon, paid on your full bid. I spend 3 more; the otter, 1.

Then place workers. Combined we bid four — that's less than the card's five icons, so everyone gets exactly what they bid.

But suppose I bid four and the otter three. Seven workers, but only five icons on the card: that's an overbid of two. Subtract that from each bid: I take two, the otter takes one. The extra workers go back to the players, but their fish remain spent.

Uncovered icons left? The card drifts one slot downstream — into costlier water. Cards only move when players auction them, so what you auction shapes the prices everyone pays.

Workers don't score on their own. To build a structure, pick your workers up off the river and spend the materials underneath them. Each structure scores victory points — and gives you benefits throughout the rest of the game.

River Bankers — merging the light fun of a worker placement game with the strategic depth of a smart economic game.
