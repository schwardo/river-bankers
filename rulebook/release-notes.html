Version v0.2 (July 2026)

A polish pass driven by The Game Crafter Sanity Test (77/100, reviewed by Chris Aylott) — sharpening the rulebook and components rather than the rules. More changes are landing in this version; this entry will grow as they do.

Why: the sanity review praised the theme and presentation but flagged concrete fixes — a sideways component picture, a rulebook that wants a table of contents and a summary-of-play up front, and a few spots that could use a diagram. v0.2 works through that list.

Components & rulebook

  • Auction chit picture fixed. The chit’s river-scene art was showing sideways in the component list; it now displays upright and is sized to scale next to the material card.
  • Labeled card-anatomy diagrams. The rulebook now calls out the parts of a structure card (title, VP block, cost row, effect text) and a material card (name, material & icon count, item icons, player-count badge, effect), each with numbered markers and a key.
  • Icon legend added to the Quick reference — the four worker chits and the six material icons, named.
  • Every component is now pictured. Added the two that had text only — the fish-track pawn (front and its “+60” flip side) and the blank chit.
  • “How the game plays” overview. A short summary of the flow — fish-track turn order, one action a turn, spend materials to build for VP, finish-line ending — now sits right before the detailed rules, so readers are oriented before the specifics.
  • Thematic cover intro. The cover’s mechanical tagline is replaced with a purely thematic blurb — four riverbank builders racing to raise the grandest works — with no game terms.
  • Full setup diagram. A new “Your table at the start” illustration shows the whole laid-out table — material deck and three revealed Headwaters cards, an empty river, the structure deck with all pawns stacked on space 0, and each player’s workers, hand, and drafted starter — every component keyed to a legend.
  • Endgame section made prominent. It now starts on its own page under a distinctive 🏁 banner header, so tired players can find the end-of-game rules at a glance.
  • A visual play example. A three-step “worked auction” — a card comes up, secret bids are revealed, then everyone pays and places while the card drifts downstream — now illustrates the core loop in pictures.
  • Table of contents & page numbers. A short Contents box on the first page, and a page number on every page, make sections easy to find. Cross-references now point to real page numbers instead of “at the back” / “next page.”
  • Glossary. A short alphabetical glossary of the game’s terms (Headwaters, graduate, jam, tableau, …) near the back.
  • Consistent wording. One term per thing now: the fish-track piece is always the pawn, river cards slide, and the pieces you bid with are worker tokens.

Version v0.1.9 (June 2026)

A new endgame, a fresh batch of card tweaks, and one new structure. The headline is the endgame overhaul — the rest follows from it.

The endgame, reworked the big change

Why: in the 4-player playtest the game felt like it should wrap up about when the material deck emptied — but the old snap-to-retirement ending dragged on past that point. The new finish line gives players a clear target to race toward and a clean wind-down.

Before. The endgame began when the material deck emptied: all pawns snapped to spaces 1, 2, 3…, then play continued until each pawn reached space 59 (or passed) and retired. The fish track also punished over-spending with lapping — sprint a full lap past another pawn and you flipped over, "exhausted" and out of turns until they caught up.

After. The game now ends on a finish line, not on the deck:

  • The line is a single flat 90 fish — the same at every player count, sitting on the second lap (the "+60" side, space 30). The game ends by pawns crossing it rather than by the board running dry, and it lands a game at roughly 30 min (2P) / 40 (3P) / 55 (4P).
  • The deck running out does not end the game — you keep auctioning the river and Headwaters cards already on the board until pawns reach the line. But once the material deck is empty, the active player drifts +1 fish at the end of each turn, so the finish line rushes up and the dead-board tail can't drag.
  • The instant your pawn reaches the line, you retire. Once the first player crosses, everyone else may Retire on their turn instead of acting, sliding to the lowest open space at or past the line. No two pawns share a finish space: if you land where a retired pawn already sits, slide forward to the next open space.
  • When all have retired, each player makes one last build, then you score. Ties break to the lower final fish position.
  • Lapping / exhaustion is gone. Instead, flip your pawn chit to its "+60" side each time you pass 0, so your true track position stays readable across laps.
  • Invent now costs at least 2🐟 (choose N = 2–5, was 1–5). A player with nothing to do advances to the finish line faster, which cuts the slow "draw one, inch forward" grind at the end without changing how much anyone builds.

New card — Confluence

Confluence (Reeds 2 / Stones 2 · time 3 · 5★) — As an action: pay 🐟 to run a single auction over two material cards with the same item type (Headwaters or river), pooling all their uncovered icons into one larger pool; both cards then float downriver. A way to force a big, contested grab.

New card — Bramble Shoal 2-player only

Bramble Shoal (Stones / Vines ×5) — a shared wildcard where each claimed icon yields Stones or Vines, chosen at build time. It appears only in 2-player games: at that count the separate Stones-5 and Vines-5 cards (Rocky Shoal and Trailing Vine) come out of the deck and this single card takes their place, adding contention at the count where the material market otherwise thins out and completing the set of three wildcard pairs (logs/reeds, clay/mud, stones/vines). At 3+ players the deck is unchanged.

Card changes before → after

The new endgame caps laps (you pass 0 at most once or twice), which gutted the "At 0 on 🐟 track" cards — they could fire only at the very end. They've been reworked into once-per-game abilities; Mill Wheel's old clamp would have blocked you from finishing, so it became a copy-your-neighbour card.

A few cards were also renamed to stop their titles colliding with the four species names — the beaver hand-size starter is now Beaver Cache (it stacks with the main-deck Cache Burrow), and several generic structures shed their animal prefixes. Cosmetic only; no rules change.

Components

  • Pawns. Each species now has a dedicated fish-track pawn — a chit with a "+60" lap back (flip it when you pass 0). It's used only on the fish track, not as a worker.
  • Blank chits: 41 (up from 28), to cover the 90th-percentile of a busy game.
  • New auction chit. Place it on the card up for auction so everyone can see what they're bidding on.
  • Per-species structure-card backs (drawn from each species' worker icon).
