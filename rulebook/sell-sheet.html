The river brings the materials. You bring the ambition.
An engine builder built on a novel, multi-unit auction mechanic — blind-bid workers for materials on cards drifting downriver, and spend them to build structures along the riverbank.
2–4 Players 30–60 Min Ages 10+ Family Strategy

The hook — a multi-unit auction that's genuinely new

Each material card shows a row of item icons at a posted per-item price. Every player secretly bids how many items they want, then all reveal at once. Add up the bids and subtract from the number available — that's the overbid.

  1. Plenty to go around (overbid = 0) — everyone takes exactly what they bid for. Done.
  2. River jam (overbid > 0) — subtract the overbid from each player's bid to see what they won; the price ticks up and the card slides one step downriver to be fought over again.

Bids are capped by the workers in front of you, so overspending is impossible — no change-making, no math. The river does the price discovery on its own.

How it plays

On your turn, bid workers on a material drifting down the river, or build a structure from your hand by paying its materials. Every action moves you along the fish track; whoever sits furthest back goes again. When the fish run out, the grandest builder on the bank wins.

Features

  • Turn order you buy and spend Actions are paid in fish on a shared 60-space track that is the turn order — furthest-back always acts next, so a cheap move can hand you two turns in a row.
  • Asymmetry and a deck full of combos An optional species draft gives each of the four mammals a distinct opening power. A deck of ~50 structure cards, each with its own effect, keeps every game different.
  • Charming and low-cost to produce Cards, wooden workers, two small boards, a warm nature theme — broad shelf appeal on a friendly bill of materials.

In the box

  • 1 river board & 1 fish-track board
  • 25 material cards · 49 structure cards
  • 12 species starter cards
  • 32 wooden worker meeples (8 per species)
  • 4 fish-track pawns · 42 chits · rulebook
